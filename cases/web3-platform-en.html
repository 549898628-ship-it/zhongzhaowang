Case Study 03

Web3 Institutional Digital Asset Trading Platform

This was a 0-to-1 platform build for a Hong Kong licensed digital-asset trading business, focused on capital flows, risk interception, confirmations, and trust-building.

Snapshot

  • Role: Lead UX owner
  • Type: HK licensed Web3 platform
  • Stage: 0 to 1
  • Focus: funding, deposit, withdrawal, orders

Context

PantherTrade was a Hong Kong licensed institutional digital-asset platform built from scratch. The product needed to support complex, high-risk financial operations while staying compliant and understandable.

My role

As the lead UX owner, I shaped the platform experience architecture and designed the core money-related flows under strict regulatory and operational constraints.

Problems I focused on

  • Information architecture and interaction patterns for both novice and expert users.
  • Funnel and friction analysis across the full transaction journey.
  • Trust-building through confirmation design, progress clarity, and compliance cues.

Key design actions

  • Mapped the critical nodes in a deposit and withdrawal journey to define what users needed to understand at each step.
  • Clarified the three most important things on confirmation pages: key risks, operating conditions, and expected results.
  • Added smart front-loaded interception, such as automatic chain-format recognition for withdrawal addresses.
  • Designed address-whitelisting rules and staged verification strategies, including cooling-off periods for new addresses.
  • Added multi-party review and early warning mechanisms for high-value transfer scenarios.

Interface principles

  • Create a clear information path that guides users through critical transaction steps.
  • Emphasize essential information and reduce redundant content into simpler structured lists.
  • Separate primary and secondary modules so users can judge action hierarchy more confidently.

Outcome

The Figma content states that the project established Web3-specific design standards, helped ship the platform on time, and addressed complex pain points such as address management and multi-party review while improving transparency and safety in capital-flow interactions.
