Case Study 02

KA Portal Institutional Asset Management Platform

This case is primarily about platform architecture. The key challenge was not improving one page, but creating a coherent experience structure across many modules, roles, and task types.

Open Resume Previous Case

Figma Visual Pages

The frames below are shown directly from the portfolio export.

Snapshot

  • Role: UX Architecture Lead
  • Type: Large B2B platform
  • Scope: 8 modules, 200+ pages
  • Outcome: 85% component reuse

Context

KA Portal served licensed financial institutions across securities, trading, reporting, and asset-management workflows. The platform had to support expert users and complex tasks while remaining learnable and scalable.

As the system expanded, inconsistencies across modules increased user learning cost, engineering effort, and maintenance burden.

Problem definition

  • The product had broad coverage, but the structure was not consistent enough across modules.
  • Institutional and wealth-management scenarios required speed, precision, and confidence.
  • Without a stable platform framework, future growth would amplify complexity.

Actions

  • Built unified page skeletons, navigation logic, and task structures.
  • Redesigned trading, reporting, and task flows to reduce switching cost.
  • Scaled an enterprise B2B component library for cross-module reuse.
  • Treated design decisions as platform-level investments rather than page-level outputs.

System content explicitly shown in Figma

  • Permission system based on RBAC, supporting 30+ configurable permission points.
  • Role-based interfaces where admins, traders, and risk users see different views.
  • Sensitive information handling such as masked totals and customer data redaction.
  • Separation between operational permissions and data permissions.

Component system breakdown

  • Trading components: order panels, position lists, order books, order details, trade history.
  • Risk components: margin monitoring, risk alerts, liquidation warnings, limit management.
  • Data components: live quotes, candlestick charts, intraday charts, asset pie charts, return curves.
  • Form components: finance-specific inputs for amount, quantity, and price.
  • Compliance components: risk disclosures, suitability prompts, confirmations, and approval flows.

Reasoning

  • In platform products, the main long-term risk is often structural instability, not isolated visual weakness.
  • As module count and role count grow, inconsistent interaction patterns become learning and collaboration costs.
  • A component library matters only if it truly serves high-frequency scenarios and repeatable structures.

Outcome

  • Served 50+ licensed financial institutions.
  • Covered 80+ billion HKD in managed asset scenarios.
  • Reached 85% component reuse and improved engineering collaboration efficiency by 40%.

This project best represents my ability to create structural clarity and long-term reuse for platform products.
