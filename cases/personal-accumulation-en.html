Using 5W2H as an early-stage “scalpel”

  • The Figma text explicitly says that in early project exploration, 5W2H is a “scalpel” used to dissect problems.
  • That suggests your starting point is not interface output, but problem framing, goal definition, and constraint clarification.

Aligning direction before execution

  • The Figma text explicitly says that defining the problem and planning ahead helps avoid blind execution and keeps design direction aligned with product goals.
  • The listed preconditions include business goals, user pain points, development resources, design timeline expectations, and expected ROI.

From exploration to output

  • The same section includes the labels “exploration stage,” “output stage,” and “5W2H,” which implies the method is meant to bridge analysis and delivery.
